Web Safety

We should all be browsing the web safely and securely. Here are some tips for safe web browsing:

  • Keep your browser software up to date. Browser updates include patches which can close vulnerabilities.
  • Use anti-virus software. You should also avoid downloading from sites whenever possible.
  • Watch out for phishing. Don't give out personal info when solicited by email/social media messaging. Be careful to read the web address in the browser to make sure it matches with where you actually wanted to go.
  • Don't reuse passwords. Several "secure" sites have been caught storing passwords in plaintext or unsalted hashes. Use different passwords for different sites. Strong passwords are very long; I use a combo of 3-5 words and 1-5 numbers. I will use a symbol occasionaly too.
  • Use an ad-blocker & enable your pop-up blocker. U-Block is an excellent ad-blocker. Pop-ups can be irritating and they can be used to hide malicious activity.
  • Make sure to use 'https://' for secure browsing. Do not enter any personal information on sites without 'https://'
  • If it sounds too good to be true, it is. Scammers will try to take advantage of you.
  • Disable cookies for sites that you don't trust. Cookies can be used to track your browsing habits. Criminals can build a profile of your online activity. Cookie management programs can delete old/unwanted cookies.

Sources
